Älplermagronen

Commonly served during the cold winter season, this amazing pasta dish combines macaroni with diced potatoes, creamy Gruyere cheese sauce, and sweet applesauce!

Servings: 6

Time: 1 hour

Ingredients

  • 5 Strips of Bacon
  • 2 tbsp Butter
  • 2 Spanish Onions thinly sliced
  • Kosher Salt and Black Pepper
  • 3 Granny Smith or McIntosh Apples
  • 4 tbsp Water
  • 275 g Yukon Gold Potatoes peeled and diced
  • 300 g Ziti Pasta
  • 1 1/3 cup Table Cream
  • 1/4 cup Riesling Wine
  • 300 g Gruyère Cheese
  • 1/4 tsp Ground Nutmeg

Instructions

  1. In a nonstick skillet over medium heat add bacon strips and fry on both sides until crispy, approximately 8 minutes. Remove crispy bacon strips with tongs and cool on a paper towel-lined plate. Leave 1 tablespoon of bacon fat in then pan and discard the rest.
  2. Return the skillet to medium heat and melt the butter. Add the sliced onions, season with salt and pepper and cook, stirring regularly until crispy and golden brown, about 30 minutes. Set aside.
  3. Place the apples in a medium pot with water and cover. Simmer gently on medium-low heat, stirring once or twice, until the apple is completely tender, about 20 minutes. Mash with a fork until smooth. Set aside.
  4. Add the diced potatoes to a large pot and cover with salted water. Bring to a boil, add the macaroni and continue to cook until the macaroni is barely al dente and the potatoes are tender, approximately 7-10 minutes. Drain in a colander.
  5. Return macaroni and potatoes to the pot and add the cream and white wine over medium heat. Once the sauce is simmering add the grated cheese and freshly grated nutmeg. Keep stirring until the cheese has melted into a thick and velvety sauce.
  6. Serve the cheesy Alpine macaroni in individual bowls topped with caramelized onions, and crispy bacon lardons. Serve with applesauce on the side.
